What is Mahashivaratri?

Mahashivaratri literally means "the Great Night of Shiva". It is observed on the 14th day (Chaturdashi) of the Krishna Paksha (waning moon) in the Hindu month of Phalguna (February‚ÄìMarch).

In 2026, Mahashivaratri falls on Sunday, 15 February.

The Core Legends Behind Mahashivaratri

1. Neelkantha ‚Äì Shiva Drinks Halahala Poison

During Samudra Manthan (churning of the ocean), gods and demons churned the cosmic ocean to obtain Amrita (nectar of immortality). The first thing that emerged was Halahala ‚Äî a deadly poison that could destroy all creation.

Terrified, everyone prayed to Lord Shiva. In an act of supreme sacrifice, Shiva collected the poison in his palm and swallowed it. To prevent it from spreading through his body, Goddess Parvati immediately choked his throat, trapping the poison there. His throat turned blue ‚Äî hence the name Neelkantha.

This selfless act of compassion is the primary reason devotees fast, chant, and stay awake on Mahashivaratri ‚Äî to honor Shiva‚Äôs protection of the universe.

2. Marriage of Shiva & Parvati

After the death of Sati (Shiva‚Äôs first wife), Parvati was born as her reincarnation. She performed severe penance in the Himalayas to win Shiva as her husband.

Impressed by her devotion, Shiva accepted her. Their divine marriage is believed to have taken place on the night of Mahashivaratri ‚Äî symbolizing the perfect union of consciousness (Shiva) and energy (Shakti).

3. Shiva‚Äôs Cosmic Tandava Dance

On this night, Lord Shiva is said to have performed the Tandava Nritya ‚Äî the supreme cosmic dance that governs creation, preservation, and destruction of the universe.

The entire cosmos danced in rhythm with Shiva. Devotees remember this divine energy by staying awake, chanting, and meditating throughout the night.

4. The Infinite Jyotirlinga Pillar

Once Brahma and Vishnu argued about supremacy. An endless pillar of fiery light appeared between them. They agreed whoever found its end or beginning was superior.

Both failed. Shiva emerged from the light and said: ‚ÄúI am without beginning or end. Worship me as the Linga.‚Äù This event is linked to Shivaratri and gave rise to the 12 Jyotirlingas.

Spiritual Significance & Benefits

Mahashivaratri is not just a festival ‚Äî it is a night of deep spiritual opportunity. The veil between the material world and divine consciousness is thinnest.

  • Removes sins and ignorance
  • Helps overcome ego and negative karma
  • Grants inner peace and mental clarity
  • Awakens the third eye (spiritual vision)
  • Leads toward moksha (liberation)

Sincere fasting, mantra japa, meditation, and staying awake in jagran on this night are considered extremely powerful for transformation and divine grace.

How Devotees Observe Mahashivaratri

Traditional practices include:

  • Fasting (Vrat): Full or partial fast with fruits, milk, sabudana, and falahar food
  • Night Vigil (Jagran): Staying awake all night chanting and meditating
  • Abhishekam: Bathing Shiva Lingam with milk, curd, ghee, honey, water
  • Mantra Japa: Chanting ‚ÄúOm Namah Shivaya‚Äù 108 or more times
  • Offering Belpatra: Offering bilva leaves (very dear to Shiva)
  • Temple Visit & Aarti: Special midnight aarti in Shiva temples
